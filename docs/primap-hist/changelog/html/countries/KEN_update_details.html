Notes on data changes

Here we list notes explaining important emissions changes for the country. ’' means that the following text only applies to the TP time series, while  means that it only applies to the CR scenario. Otherwise the note applies to both scenarios.

  • 1.A, CO2: CDIAC 2023 shows a persistent growth of emissions after 2017 while the EI data used in v2.5 shows declining emissions in 2019 and 2020. This leads to higher 2022 and cumulative emissions in PRIMAP-hist v2.5.1
  • 3.A, CH4: FAO emissions have changed for 2022 and 2021 data have been added. This leads to emissions changes for 2020 - 2022. The impact on cumulative emissions is small.
  • M.AG.ELV, N2O: FAO emissions have changed for 2022 and 2021 data have been added. This leads to emissions changes for 2020 - 2022. The impact on cumulative emissions is small.
  • M.AG.ELV, CO2: Data are sourced from EDGAR. EDGAR 8.0 emissions are much higher than EDGAR 7.0 leading to much higher emissions in PRIMAP-hist v2.5.1. While the relative changes are high, the absolute impact on national total emissions is small.
